Why Go To Bicol

Bicol is a destination suitable for all with its historical sites, eco-tourism and watersports.

Located on the southern end of the island of Luzon,
Bicol is a popular destination that is rich
in culture and history.

Bicol is rich in culture and there are numerous festivals that highlight this throughout the year. Some of the festivals are of course religious based, while others showcase the uniqueness of the region.


Bicol is noted for its superb surfing, although it offers plenty of other watersports, including diving, snorkeling, kite surfing, kayaking, and swimming. There are also plenty of things to enjoy on land, like hiking, caving, and firefly watching.


The oh-so-good Bicol cuisine, just wait until you try he world-famous Bicolano dishes. Their cuisine represents the diverse cultures that make up this humble but thriving region. and has a distinct taste thanks to their abundant use of chilies and coconut cream (gata).

Frequently Asked Questions.

Weather & Climate

The climate of the Philippines is subtropical and has two seasons; the rainy season, from June to November and the dry season, from December to May.

Language

Filipino is the national language of the Philippines. However, English is widely used throughout and has become one of the official languages of the country – so don’t be shy to start a conversation!

Currency & Money Matters

The currency of the Philippines is Peso (PHP) and goes a long way when exchanging Pounds to Pesos.

Power Outlet

The most commonly used electric plug in the Philippines is the two-pin, Type A electrical plug (220V/60hz). This is different from what is commonly used in the UK.
